### 1. CP Market Microstructure
Advanced CP trading is about understanding how Saudi Aramco arrives at the 
number before it is published. The CP is not a pure spot price; it is a "spot 
market-driven term contract price" [3, 4]. 
*   **The Formulation Process:** Aramco no longer uses a tender process. 
Instead, they set the price based on their own spot sales, feedback from their 
global liaison offices, prices of competing petroleum products, and published 
spot indices (like the Argus Far East Index, AFEI) [5].
*   **The "Catch-Up" Mechanism:** The CP is set monthly, but it inherently 
contains a "catch-up" factor. Aramco will adjust the prompt month's CP to 
reflect instances where the previous month's CP deviated significantly from 
actual spot market levels [6, 7].
*   **Market Recommendations:** Before the official announcement (usually just 
prior to the end of the month for liftings in the following month [8]), market 
participants actively submit CP recommendations to Saudi Aramco. For example, 
traders might peg their recommendations for propane at $545–550/mt and butane 
slightly lower [9]. These recommendations create a pre-announcement paper 
market expectation.

### 2. CP vs Spot Market
The most exploitable inefficiency for a trader is the time-lag between the CP 
and the spot market. 
*   **The 21-Day Lag:** Data shows that the Argus AFEI is a leading indicator, 
while the Saudi CP is a lagging indicator. Specifically, the CP lags the Far 
East spot market by approximately three weeks (21 days) [10]. 
*   **Directional Exposure:** Because of this lag, traders know that term 
buyers lifting Middle Eastern LPG on a CP basis will enjoy lower prices for 
about three weeks longer in a *rising* LPG market. Conversely, they will bleed 
P&L by paying higher prices for about three weeks longer in a *falling* LPG 
market [11].
*   **Hedging the Gap:** Traders position themselves ahead of CP changes using 
CP swaps. The front month of a CP swap rolls on the very first working day that
the new CP is made available [12, 13]. If a trader anticipates the 3-week spot 
lag will force Aramco to print a lower CP, they will aggressively short CP 
paper ahead of the announcement.

### 3. CP Premium Dynamics
No one actually pays exactly "Flat CP" for physical spot cargoes. Physical LPG 
is traded at a floating premium or discount to the CP, which fluctuates wildly 
based on market stress.
*   **Standard Discounts vs. Crisis Premiums:** In a balanced market, Mideast 
Gulf spot cargoes might trade at a discount, such as -$38/mt against the CP 
[9]. However, during acute supply shocks (like infrastructure outages or the 
Hormuz conflict), immediate scarcity premiums explode. For example, during a 
recent disruption, physical cargoes for April delivery into China were assessed
at a massive CP +$254.75/mt [14].
*   **The CP-AFEI Divergence:** The premium of the Saudi CP (plus freight) 
compared to the spot AFEI is a critical metric. In recent volatile periods, the
Saudi CP+ premium to AFEI widened significantly to an extreme $83/mt [15, 16]. 
This persistent divergence indicates that the traditional CP mechanism is 
failing to reflect true marginal supply/demand balances compared to 
transparent, trade-reflective spot benchmarks like the AFEI [15, 17].

### 4. CP-Based Arbitrage
Senior traders arbitrage the structural friction between CP-linked term 
supplies and AFEI-linked spot supplies.
*   **The Chinese Shift:** Chinese buyers are highly price-sensitive and less 
security-conscious than traditional Japanese term buyers [4]. Because Aramco 
has previously held CP levels artificially high, Chinese buyers have 
aggressively retreated from CP term contracts toward CFR spot buying to avoid 
being locked into inflated prices [18, 19]. 
*   **The Hengyi/SEA Strategy:** For our desk, selling our Brunei equity 
production on a Saudi CP-related basis aligns us with traditional regional 
wholesale structures, protecting us from basis risk between domestic and 
international transactions [2]. However, if the CP premium to AFEI blows out to
$83/mt [15], our CP-priced Brunei exports become incredibly expensive for spot 
buyers in China. In this scenario, we must dynamically decide whether to hold 
our CP-linked pricing or offer our cargoes on a spot AFEI-linked basis to 
compete with discounted US Gulf Coast cargoes.

### 5. Strategic Positioning
A trader’s positioning around the CP publication date can make or break the 
desk's monthly P&L.
*   **The Pricing Shock:** In October 2025, Saudi Aramco surprised the market 
by cutting the propane CP by $25/mt to $495/mt. This created a massive $50/mt 
shortfall compared to trader expectations [20-22]. 
*   **Mark-to-Market Devastation:** Traders who had positioned for seasonal 
winter price strength were caught holding long positions. The unexpected $50/mt
shortfall triggered systematic revaluations of forward-month commitments, 
handing massive mark-to-market losses to traders unhedged against the CP 
announcement [23]. 
*   **Risk Adjustments:** Following a shock CP publication, forward curve 
structures collapse instantly (e.g., expectations deteriorating from $555/mt 
down to $454/mt) [24]. Post-publication, traders immediately reduce speculative
directional positions and transition to heavy basis hedging, focusing purely on
downside protection [25, 26].

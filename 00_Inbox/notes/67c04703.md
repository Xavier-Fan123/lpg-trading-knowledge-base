**Dynamic Hedging Architecture: 44,000 MT Physical Long (45-Day Arrival)**

Managing a 44,000 MT VLGC physical long arriving in 45 days structurally places
your pricing window into the M2 (second-forward) month [1-3]. To neutralize 
Flat Price Delta, the baseline execution requires shorting exactly 44 lots 
(1,000 MT each) of the corresponding AFEI or CIF ARA M2 swaps [4-6]. However, 
because financial swaps settle linearly against a daily calendar month average,
while your physical cargo discharges continuously over a 24-to-36 hour pumping 
window logged on the Statement of Facts (SOF), you are exposed to significant 
structural basis risk [6-9]. 

To architect this correctly in Openlink Endur, configure the `Trade Process 
Manager` to partition the 44,000 MT physical asset into a daily straight-line 
volumetric profile mapped against the `CurveType` object of the short swap. Any
deviation in physical delivery (e.g., discharging entirely in the first week of
the month) must trigger the execution of daily "physicals" (cash-settled daily 
instruments) to bridge the basis gap between the SOF timestamp and the unpriced
days remaining in the swap's averaging period [8, 10].

**Delta & Vega Management: The Flip from Contango to Steep Backwardation**

When the forward curve is in contango, the cost of carry and interest outpaces 
the convenience yield ($r + c \ge y_1$) [11]. A sudden supply shock flips the 
market into steep backwardation, implying the convenience yield of holding the 
physical commodity now massively exceeds the risk-free rate ($y > r$) [11-13]. 

Because energy commodities exhibit a profound *inverse leverage effect*—where 
price spikes are perfectly correlated with violent surges in volatility 
($\sigma$) due to inventory scarcity—your risk profile fundamentally alters 
[14-16]. 
1.  **Delta ($\Delta$)**: The prompt leg of your short swap hedge will 
hemorrhage mark-to-market losses. Under extreme backwardation, linear 
approximations fail. Your expected P&L attribution must be modeled via a Taylor
expansion in Endur’s Advanced Portfolio Management (APM) risk engine: 
$P\&L_{pred} = ESP \times Return + \frac{1}{2} \Gamma \times Return^2 + \nu 
\Delta\sigma$ [17-19]. 
2.  **Vega ($\nu$)**: While your linear swap carries zero Vega, the embedded 
delivery optionality in your physical contract (volume tolerances, destination 
flexibility) functions as an unhedged real option [20]. Due to the inverse 
leverage effect, the implied volatility smile in LPG heavily skews to the 
right, meaning out-of-the-money (OTM) calls explode in value [16]. You must 
dynamically re-hedge this exploding Vega by aggressively selling OTM calls in 
the Platts MOC eWindow to capture the volatility premium, parametrising the 
risk via Volatility Modulated Lévy-driven Volterra (VMLV) processes inside your
risk engine [21-23].

**Mathematical Calculus of the Roll Yield**

Roll yield is the fundamental P&L driver generated by the forward price 
$F(t,T)$ converging to the spot price $S(t)$ over time. The mathematical 
relationship under no-arbitrage is defined as:
$$F(t, T) = S(t)e^{(r-y)(T-t)}$$ [24, 25]

To calculate the instantaneous roll yield (assuming the spot price remains 
constant), take the partial derivative of the forward price with respect to 
time ($t$):
$$Roll\ Yield = \frac{\partial F}{\partial t} = (y - r) F(t, T)$$

In steep backwardation ($y \gg r$), the forward curve slopes violently downward
[11, 12]. Because you hold a *short* paper hedge against your physical long, 
you suffer a massive negative roll yield (basis bleed) if vessel delays force 
you to roll your short swap from M1 to M2. You are forced to buy back the 
prompt swap at a massive premium and sell the deferred swap at a steep 
discount. In Endur, this must be isolated in the `PnL Attribution` module to 
cleanly separate Flat Price P&L from Theta (time decay/roll yield) [26, 27].

**Margin Account Implications: Mark-to-Market vs. Mark-to-Model**

The bifurcation between the physical and financial legs of the book creates 
immense liquidity risk during backwardated spikes [28]. 
*   **Mark-to-Market (MtM)**: Your short AFEI swaps are cleared instruments 
subject to daily Variation Margin (VM) and Initial Margin (IM) calls [28-30]. 
Even though your 44,000 MT physical long is appreciating identically in value, 
the physical asset is illiquid and does not generate cash until the MT103 Swift
transfer clears post-discharge. The MtM losses on the short paper will 
instantly drain the desk's cash reserves [28, 29]. Endur’s Credit Risk module 
must be programmed to run Monte Carlo simulations calculating Credit VaR and 
Potential Future Exposure (PFE) to preemptively secure credit lines before the 
margin calls breach the desk's limits [31-33].
*   **Mark-to-Model**: The physical cargo’s embedded optionality (e.g., the 
right to divert from Chiba to Ningbo) lacks a liquid observable market price 
and must be Marked-to-Model [34]. Utilize Kirk’s approximation for non-zero 
strike spread options to value this logistical flexibility [34-36]. If your 
real-time P&L attribution generates a massive unexplained residual 
($P\&L_{real} - P\&L_{pred} = \epsilon$) [19, 37], it indicates your internal 
model is mispricing the convenience yield or failing to capture the tail-risk 
severity of the backwardation, requiring an immediate transition from standard 
VaR to Expected Shortfall (CVaR) [38, 39].

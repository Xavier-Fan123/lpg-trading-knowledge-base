Coming from risk management, you are used to tracking basis risk. Trading

Here is your desk training module for Episode 2.

### 1) Core Market Logic
In the East of Suez market, the Saudi CP is the absolute baseline cost of goods
for Middle Eastern LPG ("Review of the appropriateness of the current LPG 
international benchmark", Sec 4.1). Because Saudi Arabia is the dominant 
exporter, accounting for over 50% of world-traded LPG directly or indirectly 
priced relative to the CP, its monthly posted price acts as the floor (or 
ceiling) for Asian economics ("Review of the appropriateness...", Sec 4.1). For
your desk's P&L, profitability hinges on correctly predicting the spread 
between this monthly FOB benchmark and the daily CFR spot prices in China 
(AFEI). If the CP is set too high relative to Asian spot markets, term lifters 
bleed cash; if it is set low, massive arbitrage margins open up.

### 2) Key Concepts
*   **The Saudi CP:** The official selling price for propane and butane set 
monthly by Saudi Aramco. It is quoted FOB (Free On Board) Ras Tanura ("The 
Professional LPG Trader’s Knowledge Handbook", Sec: Saudi Aramco Contract 
Price; "Review of the appropriateness...", Sec 4.1).
*   **Publication Timing:** The CP is published just prior to the end of the 
month for physical liftings occurring in the *following* month ("Review of the 
appropriateness...", Table 4.2).
*   **The "Catch-up" Mechanism:** The CP methodology is proprietary but heavily
incorporates spot market movements from the previous month. It acts as a 
"lagging" indicator that catches up to market deviations ("Review of the 
appropriateness...", Sec 4.3).
*   **The Pricing Formula:** A physical cargo out of the Middle East is 
generally priced as: $CP + Freight + Premium/Discount$ ("The Professional LPG 
Trader’s Knowledge Handbook", Sec: Saudi Aramco Contract Price).
*   **The 21-Day Lag Effect:** Data shows that the Saudi CP behaves almost 
identically to the Argus Far East Index (AFEI) *lagged by 21 days*. Therefore, 
term buyers pay lower prices for roughly three weeks in a rising market, but 
higher prices for three weeks in a falling market ("Review of the 
appropriateness...", Sec 4.5).
*   **CP Swaps:** Financial OTC instruments used to hedge CP exposure. They are
assessed for forward months and roll on the first working day after the new CP 
is announced ("Argus International LPG methodology", Sec: International swaps 
prices).

### 3) How Traders Actually Use This
On the desk, you are constantly evaluating whether to fulfill Chinese butane 
demand using term CP-linked Middle Eastern supply or by buying spot cargoes 
priced off AFEI. You will track the **CP-to-AFEI spread**. If the CP premium to
AFEI widens significantly (e.g., hitting $83/mt as seen during the 2025 
tariffs), it signals that Middle Eastern term supply is structurally expensive 
relative to delivered spot alternatives ("Asian LPG Market Tariffs and Trade 
Flow Analysis", Sec: Regional Price Differential Economics). You will use CP 
swaps to lock in your FOB purchase costs while selling AFEI swaps to lock in 
your CFR destination revenues, capturing the margin in between.

### 4) Step-by-Step Trading Example
Let’s price a Middle Eastern physical cargo bound for South China to see if the
trade is profitable compared to buying on the spot market.
*(Note: All numbers are illustrative)*

1.  **Identify the Cargo Cost:** A supplier offers you a 44,000 mt spot cargo 
FOB Ras Tanura at a premium to the CP: $Saudi CP + $30/mt$ ("Review of the 
appropriateness...", Sec 3.3).
2.  **Lock the Benchmark:** You buy a current-month CP swap at $520/mt.
3.  **Add Logistics:** You secure a VLGC (Very Large Gas Carrier) from the 
Middle East to Japan/South China. Freight costs are $45/mt ("Review of the 
appropriateness...", Sec 6.4).
4.  **Calculate Delivered Cost:** $520 (CP) + $30 (FOB Premium) + $45 (Freight)
= **$595/mt**.
5.  **Compare to Destination Benchmark (AFEI):** The current forward AFEI swap 
for the delivery window is trading at $620/mt.
6.  **P&L Margin:** $620 (Destination Revenue) - $595 (Delivered Cost) = 
**+$25/mt**. 

### 5) Trader Decision Process
*   **Go:** The calculated delivered cost (CP + Premium + Freight) is 
comfortably below the AFEI forward paper price, and you can execute the 
physical deal while simultaneously executing the paper hedges (Buy CP swap, 
Sell AFEI swap).
*   **No-Go / Kill the Trade:** VLGC freight rates suddenly spike. A jump in 
freight from $45/mt to $75/mt instantly turns a $25/mt profit into a $5/mt 
loss. Alternatively, if Middle East tensions cause shipowners to demand 
exorbitant war-risk premiums for passing the Strait of Hormuz, the trade is 
killed ("US-Iran conflict: Strait of Hormuz crisis reshapes global oil 
markets", Sec: Implications for traders).

### 6) Common Trader Mistakes
*   **Hedging Physical CP with Paper AFEI:** If you buy a physical cargo priced
on Saudi CP but try to hedge it by selling AFEI swaps without accounting for 
the freight and location differentials, you are taking massive **basis risk**. 
If the CP-AFEI spread blows out, your hedge fails ("The Professional LPG 
Trader’s Knowledge Handbook", Sec: Price and Basis Risk).
*   **Misunderstanding Timing/Settlement Risks:** The CP is a single, flat 
monthly posted number, whereas physical spot sales often price against daily 
rolling averages.

### 7) Desk Exercise
**Task:** You are a trader on the SEA desk. It is late October. You hold a 
physical term contract to buy 22,000 mt of butane FOB Ras Tanura in November, 
priced at the November Saudi CP. 

Currently, the Argus Far East Index (AFEI) for November is plummeting because 
of weak petrochemical demand in China, but the October CP was set very high. 
Knowing the "lag effect" of the Saudi CP methodology, identify your primary 
timing/pricing risk for the upcoming November CP announcement and outline how 
you would hedge this physical lifting today.

### 8) Answer Framework
*   **Step 1 (Risk Identification):** Apply the "21-day lag" rule. Because CP 
is a lagging indicator that catches up to prior spot movements, acknowledge 
that the November CP might not drop as fast as the currently plummeting daily 
AFEI. You risk buying expensive CP butane and selling it into a cheap AFEI spot
market.
*   **Step 2 (The Hedge):** You must lock in your margin *now*. Detail the 
exact paper trades you need to execute (Which swap do you buy? Which swap do 
you sell?) to neutralize the flat price risk.
*   **Step 3 (Basis/Freight):** State the unhedgeable components (e.g., 
physical freight volatility or exact physical premium variations) that remain 
on your book.

### 9) Real-World Signals to Monitor
*   **Argus Middle East Index (AMEI) & Gulf CP FOB:** Tracks the spot premiums 
or discounts being assessed against the CP for FOB cargoes ("Argus 
International LPG methodology", Sec: Middle East and Africa).
*   **The 15th of the Month:** The date the Argus AFEI rolls forward to the 
next month, shifting the benchmark pricing window ("Argus International LPG 
methodology", Sec: Asia-Pacific).
*   **End of Month Saudi Announcement:** Monitor the official Aramco press 
release for the next month's CP; it instantly reprices the global term market 
("Review of the appropriateness...", Table 4.2).

# Chinese PDH Plants

## Overview

Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) plants in China are **dedicated petrochemical facilities** that convert propane into propylene, a key feedstock for polypropylene production. China's PDH sector has expanded rapidly since the early 2010s and is now the **single largest marginal demand driver** for global LPG, consuming a substantial share of China's total LPG imports exceeding **96 million tonnes annually**.

## Process and Feedstock Inflexibility

- PDH technology (typically Honeywell UOP Oleflex or Lummus CATOFIN) is designed exclusively for propane feedstock.
- Unlike steam crackers, which can switch between naphtha, ethane, and LPG feeds, **PDH plants cannot switch feedstocks**. They must consume propane regardless of price.
- This inflexibility makes PDH plants a source of **inelastic demand**, meaning they continue purchasing propane even when margins compress, until margins turn sufficiently negative to justify temporary shutdowns.

## Impact on Global LPG Pricing

- PDH plant **operating rates (run-rates)** are a critical signal for the [[AFEI Benchmark]]. When Chinese PDH margins are healthy, high run-rates pull propane imports into Asia, supporting AFEI levels.
- Conversely, widespread PDH margin compression and unit shutdowns signal demand destruction, weakening AFEI and widening the CP-AFEI discount.
- The [[Chinese PDH Margin]] (propylene revenue minus propane feedstock cost minus conversion cost) is one of the most closely tracked metrics on LPG trading desks.

## Swing Demand Role

- PDH capacity additions in China have been the **primary source of incremental global LPG demand growth** over the past decade.
- New plant commissioning announcements and turnaround schedules are leading indicators for forward LPG demand and pricing.
- China's PDH build-out has structurally linked Asian propane prices to global polypropylene markets, creating a cross-commodity transmission mechanism.

## Capacity and Outlook

- China's installed PDH capacity has grown from near-zero in 2010 to over 20 million tonnes per year of propylene output capacity.
- Additional capacity continues to be commissioned, though the pace of new builds is moderating as margins tighten and feedstock competition increases.

## Associated Risks

- **Feedstock price risk**: PDH plants are captive propane consumers with zero feedstock flexibility; sustained high propane prices (AFEI/CP) compress margins and can force shutdowns — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Demand destruction risk**: Widespread PDH margin compression triggers coordinated shutdowns, creating a sudden drop in global propane demand that weakens spot prices and disrupts trading positions
- **Supply chain disruption risk**: PDH plants depend on imported propane arriving via VLGC; any disruption to [[Strait of Hormuz]], [[Panama Canal]], or [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]] directly threatens feedstock availability — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Basis risk**: PDH margins are calculated using AFEI-referenced propane costs versus domestic Chinese polypropylene prices; currency fluctuations and benchmark mismatches create hedging exposure — see [[Physical Hedging Architecture]]
- **Overcapacity risk**: Continued PDH capacity additions in China risk structural margin erosion for the entire sector, reducing propane demand growth and altering global LPG supply-demand balances
- **Demurrage and logistics risk**: PDH plants relying on just-in-time propane delivery face operational disruption if vessel scheduling delays or port congestion interrupt supply — see [[Demurrage and Laytime]]

# Ras Tanura Terminal

## Overview

Ras Tanura is **Saudi Aramco's primary crude oil and LPG export terminal**, located on the Persian Gulf coast of Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province. It is one of the largest petroleum export facilities in the world and a critical node in global LPG supply chains. Together with the adjacent **Juaymah terminal**, Ras Tanura handles the majority of [[Saudi Aramco]]'s waterborne LPG exports.

## Facility Specifications

- Ras Tanura's port complex includes **deep-water berths** capable of accommodating VLGCs (Very Large Gas Carriers), VLCCs (crude tankers), and smaller product carriers simultaneously.
- The terminal is connected via pipeline to Saudi Aramco's gas processing plants and NGL fractionation facilities in the Eastern Province, including the massive Abqaiq and Shaybah processing complexes.
- **Juaymah**, located approximately 20 kilometers south of Ras Tanura, serves as a complementary LPG and NGL loading facility, providing redundancy and additional throughput capacity.
- Combined, the two terminals can handle loading operations for dozens of LPG cargoes per month under normal operating conditions.

## Juaymah NGL Facility

- **Juaymah** is the world's **7th-largest LPG export terminal**, with average monthly exports exceeding **450,000 tonnes** (propane/butane roughly evenly split) over the past two years.
- India absorbs at least **60% of Juaymah's annual LPG exports**; the facility supplies approximately **15% of India's total LPG imports**.
- On **23 February 2026**, structural damage occurred to the delivery segment carrying propane and butane pipelines, triggering a **force majeure declaration** on 25 February 2026.
- The incident created a projected **400,000 tonne export loss** for Feb–Mar 2026, with all March cargo acceptances from Saudi Aramco cancelled.
- The outage could remove as much as **74% of Saudi Arabian exports** through March, creating minimum shortfalls of 450,000 tonnes in Asian markets.

## Capacity

- Ras Tanura whole site: **550,000 bpd** capacity.
- Freight benchmark: latest VLGC fixture traded at **$80/mt on Ras Tanura–Chiba basis** (Feb 2026 reference).

## Strait of Hormuz Dependency

- All cargoes loaded at Ras Tanura and Juaymah must transit the **[[Strait of Hormuz]]** to reach open ocean, creating a critical chokepoint dependency.
- Approximately **95% of all Middle Eastern LPG exports** pass through the Strait of Hormuz, making any disruption to Hormuz transit a potential global supply crisis trigger.
- This geographic vulnerability underpins the **war risk insurance** and P&I (Protection and Indemnity) club coverage requirements that apply to all vessels calling at Persian Gulf LPG terminals.

## Operational Significance

- Loading schedules at Ras Tanura and Juaymah are coordinated with Saudi Aramco's monthly **term contract allocation system**, where buyers receive nominated lifting dates and volumes.
- Congestion at the terminals, weather delays (shamal dust storms), or maintenance shutdowns can cascade into voyage delays, demurrage charges, and disruptions to downstream delivery schedules.
- The terminal's operational status is monitored by cargo tracking platforms such as [[Kpler]] and [[Vortexa]] as a real-time indicator of Middle Eastern LPG supply flows.

## Associated Risks

- **Operational disruption risk**: Berth congestion, equipment failures, pipeline maintenance, and shamal dust storms can delay VLGC loadings, cascading into cargo deferrals and downstream delivery failures — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Weather event risk**: Shamal wind events and extreme heat conditions periodically disrupt terminal operations, reducing loading throughput and creating vessel queue buildups
- **Tank-top risk**: Coordination between upstream processing plant output and vessel loading schedules must prevent storage overflow; imbalances risk inventory-driven operational shutdowns — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Geopolitical risk**: Ras Tanura's location on the Persian Gulf coast makes it vulnerable to regional conflict escalation, drone/missile attacks, and maritime security threats in the [[Strait of Hormuz]] transit zone
- **War risk insurance**: Vessels calling at Ras Tanura carry war risk and P&I insurance premiums that fluctuate with regional threat levels; premium spikes add significant cost to Persian Gulf LPG cargoes
- **Demurrage exposure**: Loading delays at Ras Tanura generate demurrage charges on chartered VLGCs waiting at anchorage, directly eroding cargo margins — see [[Demurrage and Laytime]]
- **Chokepoint dependency**: All cargoes must transit the [[Strait of Hormuz]], creating an inescapable single-point-of-failure for supply continuity — see [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]]

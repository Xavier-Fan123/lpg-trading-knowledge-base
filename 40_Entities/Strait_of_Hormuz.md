## Overview

The Strait of Hormuz is the **most critical maritime chokepoint in global energy markets**, a narrow waterway between Iran and Oman connecting the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman and the open Indian Ocean. Approximately **95% of all Middle Eastern LPG exports** transit through the Strait, making it the single most consequential bottleneck for global LPG supply security.

## Geographic and Operational Characteristics

- The Strait is approximately **33 kilometers (21 miles) wide** at its narrowest point, with shipping lanes of roughly 3 kilometers in each direction separated by a 3-kilometer buffer zone.
- Inbound and outbound vessel traffic (including VLGCs loaded at [[Ras Tanura Terminal]] and Juaymah) must navigate these constrained lanes under the oversight of Oman-based traffic management.
- The shallow depths in portions of the Strait limit the draft of transiting vessels, though VLGCs typically have sufficient clearance under normal conditions.

## Geopolitical Risk

- The Strait sits between **Iran to the north** and **Oman/UAE to the south**, placing it at the center of Middle Eastern geopolitical tensions.
- Iran has periodically threatened to close the Strait in response to sanctions or military confrontation, and incidents including vessel seizures, mine deployments, and drone attacks have occurred in the vicinity.
- Any **sustained blockage or disruption** to Hormuz transit would immediately remove the majority of Middle Eastern LPG supply from global markets, triggering a severe supply crisis, price spikes, and potential force majeure declarations on term contracts.

## Insurance and Risk Mitigation

- Vessels transiting the Strait carry **war risk insurance** with premiums that fluctuate based on the current threat assessment. During periods of heightened tension, war risk premiums can spike dramatically, adding significant cost to Persian Gulf loadings.
- **P&I (Protection and Indemnity) clubs** provide hull and liability coverage that may include Hormuz-specific exclusions or surcharges during conflict periods.
- Some buyers and traders maintain contingency plans to source LPG from non-Gulf origins (US Gulf Coast, West Africa, North Sea) as a hedge against Hormuz disruption.

## Impact on LPG Markets

- Hormuz risk is embedded in the structural risk premium of Middle Eastern LPG pricing and influences the relative competitiveness of US Gulf Coast cargoes as an alternative supply source.
- The threat of disruption supports the strategic importance of [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]] planning and [[Geographical Arbitrage]] diversification away from Persian Gulf dependency.

## Associated Risks

- **Transit delay risk**: Military activity, vessel inspections, or traffic management disruptions can slow or halt vessel passages, introducing scheduling uncertainty for LPG cargoes from [[Ras Tanura Terminal]] — see [[Demurrage and Laytime]]
- **War risk insurance**: Insurance premiums for Hormuz transit fluctuate sharply with geopolitical tension; premium spikes during conflict periods can add millions of dollars in cost per voyage, directly affecting delivered LPG economics
- **Tonne-mile inflation risk**: A sustained Hormuz closure would force Middle Eastern LPG to seek alternative pipeline or routing options (if available) while Asian buyers pivot to USGC supply, dramatically increasing global tonne-mile demand — see [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]]
- **Geopolitical risk**: Iran's proximity and periodic threats to close the Strait create a persistent risk premium in Middle Eastern LPG pricing; escalation to actual closure would trigger force majeure cascades across term contracts — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Supply security risk**: With approximately 95% of Middle Eastern LPG exports transiting Hormuz, any disruption removes the majority of non-US global LPG supply from the market, creating extreme price dislocation
- **Basis risk**: Hormuz disruption risk is asymmetrically embedded in the CP-AFEI spread and the FEI-Mont Belvieu arbitrage; sudden materialisation of this risk invalidates hedging assumptions — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Contingency sourcing risk**: Alternative supply from USGC is constrained by [[Panama Canal]] capacity, terminal throughput, and available VLGC tonnage, limiting the market's ability to offset a Hormuz disruption — see [[Physical Hedging Architecture]]

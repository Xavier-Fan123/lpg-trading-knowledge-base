## Overview

Mont Belvieu, Texas, is the **primary pricing hub for US natural gas liquids (NGLs) and LPG**. Located approximately 30 miles east of Houston, the hub hosts the largest concentration of NGL fractionation, storage, and pipeline infrastructure in the world. Mont Belvieu prices serve as the anchor for US Gulf Coast LPG export economics and are a critical input to [[Geographical Arbitrage]] calculations between the US and Asia.

## Infrastructure

- The hub is dominated by **Enterprise Products Partners**, which operates the largest NGL terminal complex including underground salt cavern storage, fractionation trains, and pipeline connections.
- Additional operators include Targa Resources, ONEOK, and Energy Transfer, all feeding into or drawing from Mont Belvieu storage.
- Pipeline connections link Mont Belvieu to the Permian Basin, Eagle Ford, Marcellus/Utica (via Mariner East and ATEX), and Gulf Coast export docks.
- The hub connects directly to [[US Gulf Coast Export Terminals]] that load VLGCs for Asia-bound and Europe-bound cargoes.

## Pricing

- **OPIS (Oil Price Information Service)** publishes daily spot assessments for Mont Belvieu propane, normal butane, isobutane, natural gasoline, and ethane.
- Mont Belvieu propane (non-TET and TET, referring to Texas Eastern Transmission pipeline access) is the primary US LPG benchmark.
- **Conway, Kansas** serves as the secondary US NGL hub, typically trading at a discount to Mont Belvieu due to logistics constraints.
- The **FEI (Far East Index) minus Mont Belvieu spread** is the key metric for US-to-Asia LPG arbitrage viability.

## Market Significance

- US shale production growth has made Mont Belvieu the marginal pricing point for global LPG supply.
- Export terminal capacity expansions along the Gulf Coast have tightened the link between Mont Belvieu prices and Asian delivered benchmarks.
- Mont Belvieu inventory levels are a closely watched indicator of US LPG supply-demand balance, directly impacting [[LPG Supply Chain Structure]] dynamics.

## Associated Risks

- **Operational disruption risk**: Pipeline outages, fractionation plant shutdowns, or salt cavern integrity issues at Mont Belvieu can disrupt NGL supply to [[US Gulf Coast Export Terminals]], cascading into export delays and cargo deferrals
- **Weather event risk**: Hurricane season (June-November) threatens Gulf Coast infrastructure including pipelines, storage, and connecting export docks, potentially forcing extended shutdowns — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Tank-top risk**: Excessive inventory builds in Mont Belvieu storage can depress local prices and widen the USGC-Asia arbitrage, while storage shortfalls during high demand create supply squeezes — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Basis risk**: The FEI-minus-Mont Belvieu spread is the key arbitrage metric; divergence between Mont Belvieu pricing and delivered Asian values creates hedging mismatches — see [[Physical Hedging Architecture]]
- **Infrastructure concentration risk**: Dominance of Enterprise Products Partners in terminal operations creates single-operator dependency for a significant share of US NGL throughput
- **Freight linkage risk**: Mont Belvieu export economics are directly sensitive to [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]] and [[Panama Canal]] transit costs, which can erode arbitrage margins

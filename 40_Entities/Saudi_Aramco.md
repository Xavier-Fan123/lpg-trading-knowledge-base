## Overview

Saudi Aramco is the world's largest integrated oil and gas company, wholly owned by the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. In the global LPG market, Aramco functions as the single most influential price-setter through its monthly **Contract Price (CP)** mechanism for propane and butane. The CP is the benchmark reference price for virtually all term-contract LPG cargoes originating from the Middle East and is widely used across Asia as a settlement basis.

## Contract Price Mechanism

- The CP is announced **retroactively** at the end of each loading month, meaning buyers commit to lifting cargoes before knowing the final price.
- This retroactive structure creates inherent price risk for buyers and underpins much of the hedging activity in LPG derivatives markets.
- The CP is informed by, but not mechanically linked to, the [[AFEI Benchmark]] published by [[Argus Media]].
- Term contract holders receive monthly **allocation volumes** based on historical lifting patterns and bilateral agreements.

## Export Infrastructure

- **[[Ras Tanura Terminal]]** and **Juaymah** are the primary LPG loading terminals on the Persian Gulf coast.
- Combined, these facilities handle millions of tonnes of propane and butane annually, loading VLGCs and smaller vessels.
- All exports transit the [[Strait of Hormuz]], creating a single-point-of-failure geopolitical risk for global LPG supply.

## Market Role

- Aramco's production decisions and CP levels directly influence Asian spot markets, PDH plant economics, and [[Geographical Arbitrage]] between the US Gulf Coast and the Middle East.
- Term contract allocations are highly prized; losing allocation is a significant commercial penalty for traders.
- The CP-to-AFEI spread is a core metric in LPG trading desks worldwide.

## Associated Risks

- **Supply disruption risk**: Aramco's concentrated export infrastructure at [[Ras Tanura Terminal]] and Juaymah creates single-point-of-failure exposure; any operational outage removes significant global LPG supply — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Geopolitical risk**: All Aramco LPG exports transit the [[Strait of Hormuz]], exposing buyers to conflict-driven supply interruptions, war risk insurance spikes, and potential force majeure events
- **Production decline risk**: OPEC+ quota adjustments and reservoir management decisions can reduce associated gas output and LPG availability, tightening term contract allocations
- **CP pricing risk**: The retroactive Contract Price mechanism forces buyers to commit volumes before knowing the final price, creating inherent basis exposure — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Freight and routing risk**: Disruptions to the [[Panama Canal]] or Strait of Hormuz alter tonne-mile economics for competing US cargoes, indirectly shifting Aramco's competitive position — see [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]]
- **Demurrage exposure**: Loading delays at Ras Tanura due to congestion or weather (shamal storms) trigger demurrage charges on chartered VLGCs — see [[Demurrage and Laytime]]

## Overview

Petronas (Petroliam Nasional Berhad) is **Malaysia's national oil and gas company**, wholly owned by the Malaysian government. It is one of the largest integrated energy companies in Southeast Asia and a significant player in global LNG and LPG markets. Petronas's LPG operations are closely tied to its upstream gas production and LNG processing facilities, particularly in Sarawak (East Malaysia).

## Bintulu LNG/LPG Complex

- The **Bintulu complex** in Sarawak is Petronas's flagship gas processing and liquefaction facility, housing the PETRONAS LNG Complex (PLC) with multiple LNG trains.
- LPG (propane and butane) is produced as a **byproduct of LNG processing**, extracted during the gas treatment and liquefaction stages.
- Bintulu LPG output is exported regionally, primarily to Northeast Asian buyers and Southeast Asian domestic markets.

## Production Outlook

- Petronas faces **declining production from mature offshore fields** in Peninsular Malaysia and parts of Sarawak/Sabah.
- The **Jerun field development** (deepwater gas field offshore Sarawak) is one of several projects aimed at offsetting natural decline rates and sustaining gas and associated liquids production.
- Despite new field developments, the overall trajectory for Malaysian domestic production is one of gradual decline relative to growing regional demand.

## Southeast Asia Net Importer Transition

- Southeast Asia as a region is projected to transition to **net LPG importer status by approximately 2027**, driven by declining indigenous production (including from Petronas) and rising petrochemical and residential demand.
- This structural shift has significant implications for [[LPG Supply Chain Structure]], as the region will increasingly compete with Northeast Asia for Middle Eastern and US cargoes.

## Relationship to Regional Players

- Petronas has a strategic relationship with [[Hengyi Industries]], whose PMB refinery in neighboring Brunei adds downstream processing capacity to the region.
- As regional supply tightens, the interplay between Petronas's declining output and Hengyi's expanding refinery complex will shape Southeast Asian LPG trade flows.

## Associated Risks

- **Production decline risk**: Mature offshore field depletion in Peninsular Malaysia and Sarawak/Sabah is reducing LPG byproduct output, with new field developments (Jerun) insufficient to fully offset natural decline rates
- **Supply disruption risk**: Upstream operational issues (platform shutdowns, pipeline outages, processing plant turnarounds) at Bintulu can interrupt regional LPG supply — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Geopolitical risk**: South China Sea territorial disputes and potential maritime security incidents could affect shipping lanes servicing Petronas's East Malaysian export facilities
- **Regional import dependency risk**: Southeast Asia's transition to net LPG importer status by 2027 means declining Petronas output amplifies the region's exposure to volatile international pricing and freight costs — see [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]]
- **Basis risk**: Petronas LPG is priced relative to regional benchmarks; as the region shifts to import parity pricing, basis relationships between local and international benchmarks will evolve — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Hedging complexity**: Byproduct LPG volumes tied to LNG production schedules create uncertain supply profiles that complicate forward hedging — see [[Physical Hedging Architecture]]

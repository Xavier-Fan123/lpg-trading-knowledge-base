# Trafigura

## Overview

Trafigura is one of the world's **largest independent commodity trading houses**, headquartered in Singapore with major offices in Geneva, Houston, and other global trading centers. The company trades in crude oil, refined products, LPG, LNG, metals, and minerals, ranking alongside [[Vitol]] and Gunvor as a top-tier physical energy trader.

## LPG Trading Activities

- Trafigura operates a **global LPG trading book** covering propane, butane, and NGL products across all major trade routes.
- The company sources LPG from Middle Eastern term contracts, US Gulf Coast spot and term volumes, and other origins including West Africa and the North Sea.
- Trafigura's LPG desk manages physical cargoes in conjunction with paper hedging positions, optimizing the portfolio across geography, time, and product specifications.

## Logistics and Storage

- Trafigura has invested in **downstream storage and distribution infrastructure** through its Puma Energy subsidiary (which was partially divested) and direct terminal investments.
- The company manages a significant **chartering and freight book**, including VLGC time charters and spot fixtures that interact with physical cargo positioning.
- Trafigura's global storage network provides optionality to hold or redirect cargoes in response to shifting [[Geographical Arbitrage]] dynamics.

## Market Role

- Trafigura is a regular participant in LPG price assessment processes run by [[Argus Media]] and [[S&P Global Platts]], contributing to benchmark price formation.
- The company's trading flows are tracked by analytics platforms such as [[Kpler]] and [[Vortexa]], providing market intelligence on cargo movements.
- Trafigura's operational scale makes it a significant counterparty in both physical and derivatives LPG markets.

## Strategic Position

- Trafigura has expanded its **gas and LNG trading** capabilities, which complement its LPG operations through shared infrastructure, market intelligence, and customer relationships.
- The company's integrated approach to oil, gas, and NGL trading positions it to capture cross-commodity optimization opportunities.

## Associated Risks

- **Counterparty credit risk**: Trafigura transacts with a wide range of counterparties across physical and derivatives LPG markets; default by buyers, sellers, or clearing members creates financial and operational exposure — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Liquidity risk**: The company's large physical and paper LPG positions require continuous access to trade finance facilities, bank credit lines, and clearing margin capacity; funding disruptions can force position liquidation at unfavourable prices
- **Basis risk**: Multi-geography sourcing (Middle East term, USGC spot, West Africa, North Sea) and diverse hedge instruments create potential mismatches between physical and paper exposures — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Freight and routing risk**: Trafigura's VLGC charter book and cargo routing decisions are exposed to volatile freight rates and transit disruptions at the [[Panama Canal]] and [[Strait of Hormuz]] — see [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]]
- **Operational and demurrage risk**: High cargo throughput across multiple loading and discharge ports generates systematic demurrage exposure — see [[Demurrage and Laytime]]
- **Mark-to-market and margin call risk**: Large derivatives positions on [[CME Group]] and bilateral markets carry margin volatility that can strain liquidity during price dislocations — see [[Physical Hedging Architecture]]

## Overview

S&P Global Platts (now branded as S&P Global Commodity Insights) is one of the world's leading price reporting agencies (PRAs), operating across oil, gas, LNG, LPG, petrochemicals, metals, and agriculture. In LPG markets, Platts provides benchmark price assessments and operates the **eWindow** electronic trading platform as part of its Market on Close (MOC) process.

## LPG Benchmark Assessments

- Platts publishes daily assessments for propane and butane across key delivery locations including CIF Japan/Korea, FOB Arab Gulf, and FOB US Gulf Coast.
- The **Platts LPG MOC process** is a structured assessment window during which market participants submit bids, offers, and trades through the eWindow platform.
- eWindow provides a transparent, auditable record of price formation, distinguishing it from purely survey-based approaches.
- Platts also publishes the **Southeast Asia Marker (SEAM)** for LNG, which has indirect relevance to LPG through fuel-switching dynamics.

## Market Position vs. Argus

- In Asian LPG markets, the [[AFEI Benchmark]] published by [[Argus Media]] has become the dominant spot price reference, particularly for derivatives settlement.
- Platts remains influential in crude oil (Dated Brent) and refined products, providing a comprehensive energy pricing ecosystem that LPG traders reference for cross-commodity analysis.

## Methodology and Governance

- Platts assessments follow IOSCO-compliant methodologies with published criteria for data inclusion, outlier rejection, and editorial judgment.
- The eWindow MOC process typically runs during a late-afternoon assessment window (Singapore time for Asian LPG), collecting executable bids and offers.
- Platts editorial staff have discretion to adjust assessments based on market intelligence when transactional data is thin.

## Data Products

- Platts data feeds are widely integrated into trading systems, risk management platforms, and contract settlement mechanisms across the LPG value chain.

## Associated Risks

- **Benchmark manipulation risk**: The eWindow MOC process relies on participant submissions; coordinated or misleading bidding behaviour could distort assessed prices used in physical and derivatives settlement — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Data reliability risk**: Editorial discretion in adjusting assessments when transactional data is thin introduces subjectivity; assessments may not reflect executable market levels during illiquid periods
- **Regulatory and compliance risk**: IOSCO-compliant methodologies require ongoing governance; regulatory changes or enforcement actions against PRAs could alter benchmark calculation methods and affect existing contract economics
- **Basis risk**: Contracts referencing Platts assessments may diverge from actual achievable prices, particularly when Platts and [[Argus Media]] AFEI assessments differ, creating cross-benchmark basis exposure — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **System and operational risk**: Integration of Platts data feeds into trading and risk systems creates dependency; data outages or erroneous publications can disrupt mark-to-market processes and hedging workflows — see [[Physical Hedging Architecture]]

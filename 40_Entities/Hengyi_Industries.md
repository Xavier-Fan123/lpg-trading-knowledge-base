## Overview

Hengyi Industries is a Chinese-owned integrated refining and petrochemical company operating the **Pulau Muara Besar (PMB)** refinery complex in Brunei. The PMB project represents one of the largest foreign direct investments in Brunei's history and is a significant downstream processing facility in Southeast Asia, with direct implications for regional LPG supply-demand balances.

## PMB Refinery Complex

- **Phase 1** commenced operations with an 8 million tonnes per year crude processing capacity, producing refined fuels, aromatics (PX/benzene), and petrochemical intermediates.
- **Phase 2 expansion** targets approximately **20 million tonnes per year** total capacity by 2028, which would make it one of the largest refining complexes in Southeast Asia.
- Propane and butane are produced as **byproducts** of the refining and petrochemical processes, contributing incremental LPG supply to the regional market.

## Geographical Advantage

- Brunei's location provides a **logistical advantage** for serving North Asian demand centers (China, Japan, South Korea), with shorter shipping distances compared to Middle East or US Gulf Coast origins.
- This proximity reduces freight costs and voyage time, improving delivered economics relative to competing supply sources.
- The facility's output interacts with [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]] by offering a short-haul alternative to long-haul Middle East and transatlantic cargoes.

## Risk Management

- Hengyi employs **tank-top risk management** strategies to handle the operational and commercial risks of byproduct LPG production, where volumes are a function of crude throughput rather than LPG market conditions.
- Inventory management at the terminal requires coordination with vessel scheduling to avoid storage overflow scenarios.

## Strategic Context

- Hengyi's expansion occurs as [[Petronas]] faces declining production from mature Malaysian fields, with Southeast Asia broadly transitioning toward **net importer status by 2027**.
- The PMB complex is a key node in the evolving [[LPG Supply Chain Structure]] of the Asia-Pacific region.

## Associated Risks

- **Supply disruption risk**: Unplanned refinery outages or delays in Phase 2 expansion could reduce regional LPG byproduct supply, tightening Southeast Asian balances — see [[Physical LPG Contract Logic]]
- **Production decline risk**: As a byproduct producer, Hengyi's LPG output is a function of crude throughput and refinery configuration, not LPG market conditions; changes in refinery utilization directly alter LPG availability
- **Tank-top risk**: Byproduct LPG volumes must be managed against limited terminal storage capacity; vessel scheduling misalignments can create storage overflow scenarios requiring distressed sales — see [[Basis Risk Management]]
- **Geopolitical risk**: Brunei's proximity to the South China Sea introduces potential maritime security and sovereignty risks affecting shipping routes and investment climate
- **Demurrage exposure**: Coordination between refinery output timing and vessel loading windows is critical; delays at the PMB terminal generate demurrage costs — see [[Demurrage and Laytime]]
- **Freight and routing risk**: Hengyi's regional supply advantage depends on short-haul voyage economics; shifts in [[VLGC Freight Dynamics]] can alter competitive positioning relative to Middle East and USGC origins
